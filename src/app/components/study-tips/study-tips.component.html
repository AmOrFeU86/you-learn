Study Tips

Effective strategies to maximize your learning

Preview the Material

Skim through it first—check headings, subheadings, or any summaries to get the big picture. It's like scouting the terrain before diving in.

Break It Down

Split the text into manageable chunks. Tackle one section at a time instead of trying to swallow the whole thing in one go.

Active Reading

Don't just glide over the words—engage with them. Highlight key points, jot notes in the margins, or summarize what you've read in your own words. Asking yourself questions like "What's the main idea here?" keeps your brain in gear.

Connect the Dots

Tie what you're reading to stuff you already know. If it's a history text, think about how it links to events you've heard of. If it's literature, consider the characters' motivations based on what you've seen in other stories.

Review Smartly

After finishing a section, pause and recap. Quiz yourself on the main ideas or explain it to an imaginary friend (or a real one, if they're around). Teaching it back is a killer way to lock it in.

Mix Up Your Methods

Switch between reading, note-taking, and discussing it out loud. Keeps things fresh and helps it stick.
